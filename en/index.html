We come together to innovate where no one is innovating yet: in the political system. Democracia en Red is a space to imagine, design and implement innovations in the political system that can bring our democracy to the 21st century. Let’s transform noise into signal. It's time we change the tool.

About us

We’re a group of entrepreneurs and enthusiasts from different backgrounds that believe in the potential of technology when it’s put at the service of citizens. We want to motivate a new culture of participation, one where we can stop choosing between pre-set options and start designing those options. That’s why we build tools that transform the political system into a democracy with better and more diverse participation.

DemocracyOS

We aim for dOS to be the operating system of a better and more participative democracy. dOS is an open-source digital platform that allows citizens to get informed, join the conversation and vote. We develop and support the application so that other parties and organizations can use it, worldwide.

Projects

Education

Changes in society don’t happen when we adopt new tools, but new behaviours.

We need to rethink our educational system. We are still educating for an industrial society: citizens-consumers that learn only to choose between options. Recipients of a standardized knowledge conveyed vertically and penalizes them for their mistakes.

Let’s educate citizens that can design options, and that participate critically in the information-building process. These will be the citizens of an open, connected, integrated, and empathic society.

To program or be programmed.

Academia

The introduction of technological tools to political institutions requires its own library, theoretical foundations, case studies and debates.

We research different approaches, debate, and write about the concepts that define participation, deliberation, institutions and power under the paradigm of The Net, with the goal of making theoretical and empirical contributions.

Meet us

In May 2012, a group of political scientists, artists, educators, designers, programmers, and social entrepreneurs joined to deliberate about net democracy and think of ways to update our governments.

From that starting point, we set ourselves to develop a tool to carry the voice of each citizen directly to their parliament, with no middlemen, and foster the necessary social conditions for this to happen.
